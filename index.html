Information & Communication Technology System Maintenance

ICTSM is a two-year engineering trade under the Craftsmen Training Scheme (CTS), NSQF Level 4, in the IT & ITeS sector. The official curriculum defines course information, training system, job roles, learning outcomes, assessment criteria, trade syllabus, and tools/equipment lists, revised in March 2023.

Training system

The competency-based structure outlines progressive modules with clear outcomes and assessments, supporting practical readiness in ICT hardware, software, and diagnostics across two years.

Job roles

Trainees are prepared to install, operate, test, and maintain electronic equipment, personal computers and peripherals, and measuring instruments upon completion of the trade.

Study material

BharatSkills provides ICTSM trade theory and practicals (Year 1 & 2), workshop calculation & science, question banks, mock tests, and e-learning mapped to the latest syllabus versions.

Syllabus overview

Core areas include foundational electrical concepts, electronic components, PC assembly and troubleshooting, networking basics, operating systems, preventive maintenance, diagnostics, and safety practices, aligned to CTS outcomes and assessments.

Basics of electricity

Safety procedures; units and measurements; Ohm’s law; series/parallel circuits; power and energy; AC/DC fundamentals; wiring best practices; and use of basic instruments like the multimeter. These fundamentals support later modules in electronics, PC hardware, and system maintenance.

Year-wise flow

Year 1 targets foundations and essential hardware/OS tasks, while Year 2 expands into advanced maintenance, networking, diagnostics, and employability skills, with continuous assessment and practicals.

Site structure suggestion

Organize pages by year and module with outcomes, practical tasks, checklists, and links to BharatSkills materials for clarity and consistency in learning.
