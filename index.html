Information & Communication Technology System Maintenance

ICTSM is a two-year engineering trade under the Craftsmen Training Scheme (CTS), NSQF Level 4, focusing on installation, testing, maintenance, and repair of ICT systems and peripherals. It includes trade theory, trade practicals, assessment criteria, and a structured syllabus aligned to the IT & ITeS sector.

Training system

The curriculum provides competency-based modules, learning outcomes, assessment criteria, and detailed tool/equipment lists to ensure practical readiness across ICT hardware, software, and diagnostics.

Job roles

Graduates can install, operate, test, and maintain electronic equipment, computers, peripherals, and measuring instruments, supporting ICT infrastructure in varied environments.

Study material

BharatSkills hosts ICTSM trade theory, trade practicals (1st & 2nd year), workshop calculation & science, mock tests, and e-learning resources mapped to the latest syllabus versions.

Syllabus overview

The ICTSM syllabus includes foundational electrical concepts, electronic components, PC assembly and troubleshooting, networking basics, operating systems, preventive maintenance, diagnostics, and safety practices, structured over two years with defined learning outcomes and assessments.

Basics of electricity

Core topics commonly covered include safety, units and measurements, Ohm’s law, series/parallel circuits, power and energy, AC/DC fundamentals, wiring best practices, and use of basic instruments (multimeter). These underpin later modules in electronics, PC hardware, and system maintenance.

Trade theory & practical

Progressive modules blend theory with hands-on tasks across hardware assembly, OS installation, preventive maintenance, peripherals, networking, and troubleshooting, supported by structured assessment criteria and tool/equipment lists.
